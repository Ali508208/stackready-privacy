1 Introduction

StackReady ("the App", "we", "our") is a free developer utility application created to help software developers set up their development environments faster using curated, offline checklists and copy-paste-ready commands.

This Privacy Policy describes how StackReady handles information when you use our app. By using StackReady, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

Short version: StackReady does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. Everything stays on your device.
2 Information We Collect

We collect absolutely no personal information. StackReady does not ask for your name, email, phone number, location, or any other identifying information.

The app does not require you to create an account or log in at any point.

No forms, no sign-ups, no user profiles — StackReady is completely anonymous by design.
3 Local Storage (On-Device Only)

StackReady stores the following data locally on your device only using Android/iOS local storage. This data never leaves your device:

  • Your checklist progress — which setup steps you have marked as completed
  • Your bookmarked checklist items — items you have saved for quick access
  • Your theme preference — whether you use light mode or dark mode
  • Your language preference — your selected app language
  • Onboarding status — whether you have seen the welcome screens
Important: This locally stored data is only accessible by the StackReady app on your device. It is never uploaded, synced, or transmitted anywhere. You can clear this data at any time from the app's Settings screen.
4 Internet Permission

StackReady requests the INTERNET permission on Android. This permission is used solely to open external documentation links in your device's browser when you tap "View Docs" on a checklist item.

The StackReady app itself does not make any network requests, send any data to any server, or communicate with any backend service.

Opening a documentation link simply hands the URL to your phone's browser — StackReady has no involvement in or visibility into what happens after that.
5 Third-Party Services

StackReady does not integrate any of the following:

  • Analytics or usage tracking (no Firebase Analytics, no Mixpanel, no Amplitude)
  • Advertising networks or ad SDKs
  • Crash reporting services (no Crashlytics, no Sentry)
  • Social login or OAuth providers
  • Payment processors
  • Cloud sync or backup services
  • Any other third-party SDK that collects user data
6 Children's Privacy

StackReady is a general-purpose developer utility app rated for Everyone. The app does not collect any personal data from any user, including children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your country).

Since no data is collected at all, StackReady is fully compliant with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar regulations worldwide.

7 Data Security

Since StackReady does not collect or transmit any personal data, there is no personal data at risk of a security breach on our end.

All data stored locally on your device is protected by your device's built-in security mechanisms (screen lock, encryption, etc.). We recommend keeping your device's operating system up to date for best security.

8 Your Rights

Since we collect no personal data, there is nothing for us to delete, export, or correct on our end. However, you have full control over your local app data:

  • Clear all checklist progress from Settings → Clear All Progress
  • Remove all bookmarks from Settings → Clear Bookmarks
  • Uninstall the app to remove all locally stored data from your device
9 Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.

If we ever introduce a backend, account system, or any form of data collection in a future version of the app, this policy will be updated before that version is published, and users will be notified within the app.

We will never collect personal data without updating this policy and notifying you first.
